Strain Sample Submission Requirements

Please read and accept our packaging & submission requirements below. We are unable to accept improperly prepared & packaged samples.

First, prepare your bead tubes according to the MicrobesNG bead tube protocol.

It is important that any samples sent to us as strains (in bead tubes) are sent in safe and legal packaging. The applicable rules for biological samples are Packaging Instructions P650 and the UN 3373 regulations. Although there are exemptions for known non-infectious substances, we require that these rules are applied in all cases, to guard against the possibility that strain characteristics are only partially known, or that comtamination has occured.

Key packaging requirements

  • Leak-proof primary receptacle: use the bead tubes that we have provided.
  • Secondary packaging: The bead tubes should be placed into some form of sealed, leak-proof secondary packaging. A plastic container is a good choice.
  • Rigid outer packaging: The secondary packaging should be secured in rigid outer packaging (usually a strong cardboard box), surrounded by cushioning material.
  • Packaging must be capable of surviving a drop test of at least 1.2 meters.
  • Correct labeling: The return address label that we provide will already include the to & from address, so simply add a UN 3373 package marking, large enough that no side of the diamond is less than 50 mm.
  • Minimum size: At least one surface of the outer packaging must have dimensions of at least 100 mm x 100 mm.
